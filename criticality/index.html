What can we learn from a pile of sand?
As it turns out, sand piles are one of the simplest embodiments of a concept central to many complex systems: criticality.
Nothing is more sensitive than a critical system: tiny changes at a few critical points affect everything.
Sometimes this is desirable: if our brains were critical, for example, “critical" neurons in vastly different regions could “broadcast" messages.
But when neurons are too connected and too sensitive, everything fires and we end up with catastrophes: seizures, migraines, or panic attacks.
Thus, criticality is often a double-edged sword: it might enable systems to adapt suddenly, or collapse unpredictably.
